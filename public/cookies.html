You might be wondering why there's no cookie banner on ReverseQR. Here's why:

Sessions Are Temporary

ReverseQR uses temporary sessions that exist only while you're actively using the application. These sessions:

  • Expire automatically after a set period of inactivity (typically 24 hours)
  • Are deleted automatically when the session expires
  • Do not persist across browser sessions or page refreshes in most cases
  • Only store connection tokens needed for secure communication during your session

No Tracking or Advertising

We don't use cookies to track your behavior, serve advertisements, or build user profiles. Our goal is simple: facilitate secure, private communication between you and your recipients.

Compliance

Since ReverseQR:

  • Does not use cookies for tracking or marketing
  • Does not use non-essential cookies
  • Employs temporary session management only for functionality

...we don't need to ask for cookie consent. We're transparent about our approach: what we use sessions for, and nothing else.
